Setup and Requirements

The Corrate Heart Rate Training app (HRT) requires a Bluetooth heart rate device. It has been tested with the Polar H7, H9, Wahoo BLUE HR, and Garmin Forerunner 255 but should work with most Bluetooth smart devices.

Ensure Bluetooth is enabled on your iPhone, iPod, or iPad via the Control Center.

Different Views

Swipe left and right to switch between different views:

  • First View: Configure heart rate readout frequency.
  • Second View: Train using Fartlek.
  • Third View: Learn about heart rate zones and click on a zone for details.

Understanding the Display

First Row

Displays heart rate (bpm or %) and a beating heart icon. A button let you switch between displaying the heart rate in beats per minute and a percentage of your maximum heart rate. The time since the last heart rate has been received is displayed under the heart rate.

Second Row

Shows resting heart rate (HRrest), heart rate reserve (HRreserve), and maximum heart rate (HRmax). Values can be locked.

Third Row

Measures recovery heart rate (HRrecovery) with the "Start recovery" button.

Fourth Row

Controls audio readouts and interval.

Fifth Row

Controls voice speed.

Sixth Row

Displays heart rate device connection status.

Device Connection

Initial Connection

The app searches for your heart rate device automatically.

Connecting to a Different Device

Select Delete connection with device in the menu in the main view. The app will automatically search for devices once you delete the current connection. You can also start a new search by selecting Connect to device in the menu.

Notes on Heart Rate Devices

Devices may continue transmitting even when removed. Close the app completely to conserve battery.

What Does Corrate Mean?

"Cor" means heart in Latin, inspiring the name Corrate.
